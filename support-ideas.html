Support ideas

Useful ways to help without making someone manage more work.

Start with practical tasks. Ask what would help, offer specific options, and respect privacy if someone does not want to share details.

Meals and groceries

  • Drop off ready-to-heat meals in disposable containers.
  • Send shelf-stable snacks, drinks, and breakfast items.
  • Offer grocery pickup or delivery with a clear budget.
  • Ask about allergies, dietary needs, and freezer space first.

Rides and errands

  • Offer a specific ride window instead of a general "let me know."
  • Pick up prescriptions, mail, groceries, or household basics.
  • Coordinate school pickup, pet appointments, or local errands.
  • Confirm timing and mobility needs without asking for medical details.

Household help

  • Help with laundry, dishes, trash, light cleaning, or yard work.
  • Handle a single task start to finish so the person does not need to supervise.
  • Offer pet care, plant care, or package pickup.
  • Respect privacy and avoid entering rooms or areas without permission.

Gift cards

  • Choose flexible options for groceries, gas, rides, meals, or pharmacy items.
  • Include a short note with no expectation of a response.
  • Ask before sending platform-specific cards that may not work locally.
  • Keep amounts modest if it makes the gift feel easier to accept.

Care packages

  • Mix comfort items with practical supplies.
  • Include easy activities for kids if the household has children.
  • Send small items that do not create clutter or require assembly.
  • Use simple, encouraging notes instead of advice.

Organizing a group

  • Choose one coordinator so the person is not answering everyone.
  • Share only what the person has agreed can be shared.
  • Track meals, rides, and errands in a private spreadsheet or calendar.
  • Check in periodically and adjust support as needs change.
