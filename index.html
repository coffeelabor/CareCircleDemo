Community care, made practical

When you want to help someone but do not know where to start.

My Care Circle gathers simple ideas for meals, rides, groceries, household help, care packages, and local resources so families and friends can show up in useful ways.

The first version

A calm place to turn concern into action.

This proof of concept is intentionally simple. It focuses on practical, general support ideas instead of profiles, registries, payments, comments, or personal stories.

This site shares general support ideas only. It does not provide medical, legal, financial, or mental-health advice.

Common ways to help

Small tasks can lower the load.

01

Meals and groceries

Coordinate ready-to-heat meals, pantry staples, snacks, and grocery gift cards.

02

Rides and errands

Offer transportation, prescription pickup, school drop-off, or quick local errands.

03

Household support

Help with laundry, light cleaning, pet care, yard work, or organizing a home reset.

04

Care packages

Send comfort items, practical supplies, handwritten notes, or age-appropriate activities.

Article-first roadmap

Build trust through helpful content before adding bigger features.

The safest first step is publishing low-risk, practical articles that answer the questions people already search for when someone they love is in a hard season.
