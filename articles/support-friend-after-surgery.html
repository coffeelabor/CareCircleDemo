How to support a friend after surgery

Practical support ideas, not medical advice

Knowing how to support a friend after surgery often starts with ordinary tasks: meals, rides, errands, and quiet encouragement. You do not need to know private medical details to be helpful.

Offer specific help

  • Send a message with two or three concrete options, such as dinner Tuesday, grocery pickup Friday, or a pharmacy run.
  • Ask about food preferences, allergies, and drop-off timing before bringing meals.
  • Offer help that does not require a long conversation or immediate reply.

Think about the home

Light chores can be more useful than another bouquet. Laundry, dishes, trash, pet care, mail pickup, and basic tidying can keep the household from falling behind.

Respect privacy

Do not ask for medical updates unless your friend offers them. A simple note like "No need to reply, I am thinking of you" can be kinder than a list of questions.

Quick ideas

  • Ready-to-heat meals
  • Grocery pickup
  • Pet care
  • Short, low-pressure check-ins
