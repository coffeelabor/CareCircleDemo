Recovery support

Ways to help someone during recovery

General support ideas only

The best ways to help someone during recovery are often steady, practical, and respectful. Focus on what makes daily life easier, not on getting details they may not want to share.

Reduce daily decisions

  • Offer one clear task you can take on.
  • Give simple choices, such as morning or afternoon, soup or pasta, pickup or drop-off.
  • Do not expect a long explanation if the answer is no.

Coordinate with permission

If several people want to help, ask who should coordinate. One point person can prevent duplicate meals, too many visitors, and repeated questions.

Keep support flexible

Needs can change from week to week. Check whether meals, rides, errands, or quiet companionship would be most useful right now.

Good first offers

  • One meal drop-off
  • A grocery run
  • A ride to an appointment
  • A no-reply-needed message
