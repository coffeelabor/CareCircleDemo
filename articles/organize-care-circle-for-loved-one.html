Coordination

How to organize a care circle for a loved one

Simple coordination without collecting private details

A care circle is a small group of people who coordinate practical support. It works best when there is one clear coordinator and a strong respect for privacy.

Choose one coordinator

The coordinator gathers approved needs, keeps a simple schedule, and helps prevent too many messages going to the person or household receiving care.

List practical categories

  • Meals and groceries.
  • Rides and errands.
  • Household tasks.
  • Child, pet, or yard support.
  • Gift cards or care packages.

Share less, help more

Only share information the person has agreed can be shared. The group does not need diagnoses, treatment details, or personal history to provide useful help.

Simple rule

One coordinator, clear tasks, consent before sharing, and no pressure for the household to manage every helper directly.
