Rehab support

Supporting someone through rehab without overstepping

Respect, privacy, and practical help

Supporting someone through rehab without overstepping starts with privacy and consent. You can be helpful without asking for details they do not want to share.

Ask what kind of support is welcome

  • Offer rides, meals, childcare, pet care, or household help.
  • Ask whether check-ins are helpful and how often.
  • Let the person decide what they want shared with others.

Avoid advice unless invited

Encouragement and practical support are usually safer than opinions. If someone asks for professional help, point them to established resources such as SAMHSA or local providers.

Support the household too

Family members may need meals, errands, school support, or time to rest. Keep the offer concrete and respectful.

Resource note

For treatment referrals in the United States, SAMHSA lists its National Helpline at 1-800-662-HELP.
